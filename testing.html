Melbourne Storm v New Zealand Warriors, AAMI Park, 7pm

Seeing Roger Tuivasa-Sheck limp off the field last week was the most heartbreaking moment of the 2016 season so far. The Kiwis were touted as premiership contenders the moment he and Issac Luke put pen to paper but they had not clicked over the first six weeks despite RTS’ heavy involvement, averaging 197 running metres. The injection of 21-year-old excitement machine Tuimoala Lolohea at fullback is undoubtedly a curveball, considering Tuivasa-Sheck gets the fourth-most touches from fullback. Lolohea possesses much the same skill set but some divine intervention is needed to challenge the best because they have been in second gear all year, expecting miracles to materialise. But now they have to work for it, as the ‘big three’ have become two.

Meanwhile, it is a miracle that the Storm is 5-2 this season. They may have won at Leichardt Oval last week but they are playing atrocious attacking football by their lofty standards – only three teams have scored fewer points this season. They are at their best when building pressure through high completion rates, strong and direct running through the middle, and ruck domination. All three areas have been lacking in recent weeks: they are making unforced errors way out of character, halting momentum with unnecessary offloads and for the ‘wrestling champions’ they are conceding too many quick play-the-balls.
